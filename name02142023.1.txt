<section id="geralt">
  
  <p>Contact an Application Assistant.&nbsp;</p>
  <p>Survivors will be referred to an Application Assistant. Application Assistants are located throughout the state in 
  agencies and non-profit organizations that provide advocacy, counseling, referral, or shelter services to victims of 
  sexual assault, domestic violence, trafficking, or stalking. &nbsp;</p>

<h3>The Application Assistant will:</h3>

  <ul>
    <li>Explain how the ACP works</li>
    <li>Explain participant responsibilities while enrolled in ACP</li>
    <li>Work with survivors to incorporate the ACP into their overall plans</li>
    <li>Assist applicants with completing their application</li>
    <li>Forward completed application to the program</li>
  </ul>

  <p>Once an application is received, ACP program staff thoroughly review it. If the application is complete, staff then 
  certify the participants' enrollment in the program for the next four years. All certified participants are issued a 
  Washington ACP authorization card and a program handbook that details how to use the substitute address effectively with 
  government agencies.</p>


</section>
