    <h3>To be eligible for Address Confidentiality Program (ACP), an applicant must:</h3>
      <ul>
        <li><a aria-controls="collapse" aria-expanded="true" data-toggle="collapse" class="collapsesink" href="#collapse" role="button" 
        text-align="center"><font color="#000000">Be a survivor of </font><strong>domestic violence, sexual assault, trafficking, 
        or stalking</strong><font color="#000000">; OR</font></a>

          <div aria-labelledby="heading" class="button-collapse collapse kitchensink" id="collapse" role="tabpanel">
            <div class="btn-body kitchensink" align="left" id="kitchensink">
              <p><h4><u><strong>Domestic violence</strong></u></h4> Refers to an act as defined in
              <a class="linkydink" href="http://apps.leg.wa.gov/rcw/default.aspx?cite=10.99.020" target="_blank">RCW 10.99.020</a> and includes a threat of such acts committed against an individual in 
              a domestic situation, regardless of whether these acts or threats have been reported to law enforcement officers.
              <a class="linkydink" href="https://www.thehotline.org/identify-abuse/" target="_blank"><strong>>click for more information<</strong></a></p>
      
              <p><h4><u><strong>Sexual assault</strong></u></h4> Refers to an act as defined in
              <a class="linkydink" href="http://apps.leg.wa.gov/rcw/default.aspx?cite=70.125.030" target="_blank">RCW 70.125.030</a> and includes an attempt to commit such acts against an individual, 
              regardless of whether these acts or threats have been reported to law enforcement officers.
              <a class="linkydink" href="https://www.nsvrc.org/about-sexual-assault" target="_blank"><strong>>click for more information<</strong></a></p>
      
              <p><u><h4><strong>Stalking</strong></u></h4> Refers to an act as defined in
              <a class="linkydink" href="http://apps.leg.wa.gov/rcw/default.aspx?cite=9a.46.110" target="_blank">RCW 9A.46.110</a> and includes a threat of such acts committed against an individual, 
              regardless of whether these acts or threats have been reported to law enforcement officers.
              <a class="linkydink" href="https://www.stalkingawareness.org/definition-faqs/" target="_blank"><strong>>click for more information<</strong></a></p>
      
              <p><h4><u><strong>Trafficking</strong></u></h4> Refers to an act as defined in
              <a class="linkydink" href="http://apps.leg.wa.gov/rcw/default.aspx?cite=9a.40.100" target="_blank">RCW 9A.40.100</a> or an act recognized as a severe form of trafficking under&nbsp;
              <a class="linkydink" href="http://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?path=/prelim@title22/chapter78&amp;edition=prelim" target="_blank">22 U.S.C. Sec. 7102(8)</a>
              &nbsp;as it existed on June 12, 2008, or such subsequent date as may be provided by the secretary of state 
              by rule, consistent with the purposes of this subsection, regardless of whether the act has been reported to law 
              enforcement.
              <a class="linkydink" href="https://humantraffickinghotline.org/what-human-trafficking" target="_blank"><strong>>click for more information<</strong></a></p>
            </div>
          </div></li>

        <li><a aria-controls="collapse" aria-expanded="true" data-toggle="collapse" class="collapsesink" href="#collapseCJ" role="button" 
        text-align="center"><font color="#000000">Be a </font><strong>criminal justice affiliate or elections official</strong> <font color="#000000">who is a target of threats or harassment 
        as defined in RCW 9A.46.020; OR</font></a>

          <div aria-labelledby="heading" class="button-collapse collapse kitchensink" id="collapseCJ" role="tabpanel">
            <div class="btn-body kitchensink" align="left" id="kitchensink"> 
              <p><h4><u><strong>Criminal Justice Affiliates</strong></u></h4> Refers to an act as defined in
              <a class="linkydink" href="http://apps.leg.wa.gov/rcw/default.aspx?cite=9a.46.020" target="_blank">RCW 9A.46.020</a> and includes who is target of felony harassment prohibited under 
              paragraph (2)(b)(iii) or (iv), and any family member residing with him or her.&nbsp; Applicable Criminal Justice Affiliates are 
              listed under paragraph (4).</p>
            </div>
          </div>
        </li>



        <li>Be a parent or guardian acting on behalf of a minor, or a guardian acting on behalf of an incapacitated person</li>
      </ul>

      <p style="margin-left:.75in;"><strong>AND</strong></p>

      <ul>
        <li>Fear for their safety; AND&nbsp;</li>
        <li>Reside in Washington;&nbsp; AND&nbsp;</li>
        <li>Be an adult</li>
      </ul>

      <p style="margin-left:.5in;"><em>**Household members of an applicant may also be eligible.**</em></p>
      <p style="margin-left:.5in;"><em>**ACP is most effective if the applicant has recently, or is planning to, relocate to a new residence.**</em></p>
      <p>&nbsp;</p>

        <div class="btn-body" align="left" style="font-size: 16px; margin-left:.125in; font-family:Calibri (Body); font-weight: heavy;">
        <p>Anyone wishing to apply can meet with an <strong><u>Application Assistant</u></strong>.</p>
        <p>An Application Assistant is an employee of a state or local agency or non-profit organizations that provide advocacy, 
        counseling, referral, or shelter to victims. An Application Assistant must be certified by the secretary of state to 
        assist survivors.</font></p>
        


        <p>An Application Assistant will help with:</p>
        <ul>
          <li><font family="calibri"><strong>Safety planning</strong>; AND</li>
          <li><font family="calibri">Explain the details and best practices for ACP participation; AND</li>
          <li><font family="calibri">Assist applicant with filling out and submitting the application</li>
        </ul>
        <p style="margin-left:.5in;"><em>**An ACP application serves as a sworn statement, <strong>under penalty of perjury</strong>
        **</em></p>
        <br/>
        </div>
      
        <aside>
          <p>A <strong>Safety Plan</strong> is a personalized and practical plan that helps identify things a survivor can do to better protect 
          themselves and their family and to help reduce the risk of future harm.</p>

          <p style="margin-left:.25in;">It can include:</p>
          <ul>
            <li>Evaluating risks</li>
            <li>Identifying steps to reduce risks</li>
            <li>Implementing strategies</li>
            <li>Reviewing plans as needed</li>
          </ul>
        </aside>

    <h1><strong>FAQs</strong></h1>
    <div aria-multiselectable="true" class="panel-group" id="accordion" role="tablist">
      <div class="panel panel-default">
        <div class="panel-heading" id="headingA1" role="tab">
        <h4 class="panel-title"><a aria-controls="collapseA1" aria-expanded="true" data-parent="#accordion" class="collapselink"
        data-toggle="collapse" href="#collapseA1" role="button"><strong><font size="3px">&nabla;</font>&nbsp; &nbsp;How does ACP help keep my information private?</strong></a></h4>
        </div>

        <div aria-labelledby="headingA1" class="panel-collapse collapse" id="collapseA1" role="tabpanel">
          <div class="panel-body">
          <p>All participant data is classified and maintained as private data by the ACP office. Measures are in place to secure 
          participant data, which is extremely sensitive.</p>
          <p>The ACP office will only disclose a participant's real residential address to a third party in response to a valid court 
          order or law enforcement agency request. A formal request from a law enforcement agency is required to be 
          signed by the agency's chief officer<strong>*</strong>.</p>
          <p><strong>*</strong>If a participant's abuser is a member of law enforcement then a law enforcement agency would be required to 
          obtain a court order.</p></div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="panel panel-default">
        <div class="panel-heading" id="headingA2" role="tab">
        <h4 class="panel-title"><a aria-controls="collapseA2" aria-expanded="false" class="collapsed collapselink" 
        data-parent="#accordion" data-toggle="collapse" href="#collapseA2" role="button"><strong><font size="3px">&nabla;</font>&nbsp; &nbsp;Does ACP forward all types of mail?</strong></a></h4>
        </div>

        <div aria-labelledby="headingA2" class="panel-collapse collapse" id="collapseA2" role="tabpanel">
          <div class="panel-body">
          <p>The ACP forwards all First-Class mail, including registered and certified mail. First-class mail includes all personal 
          mail, bills, cards, letters, etc. The ACP will also forward bank checks. The ACP cannot forward packages, magazines, 
          or junk mail.</p></div>
        </div>
      </div>
    
      <div class="panel panel-default">
        <div class="panel-heading" id="headingA3" role="tab">
        <h4 class="panel-title"><a aria-controls="collapseA3" aria-expanded="false" class="collapsed collapselink" 
        data-parent="#accordion" data-toggle="collapse" href="#collapseA3" role="button">
        <strong><font size="3px">&nabla;</font>&nbsp; &nbsp;Can ACP participants receive registered mail or subpoenas?</strong></a></h4>
        </div>

        <div aria-labelledby="headingA3" class="panel-collapse collapse" id="collapseA3" role="tabpanel">
          <div class="panel-body">
          <p>Yes. The ACP accepts personal and legal mail. The ACP can receive such mail on behalf of a participant and forward 
          it to them. This is treated as a participant receiving the mail.</p></div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="panel panel-default">
        <div class="panel-heading" id="headingA4" role="tab">
        <h4 class="panel-title"><a aria-controls="collapseA4" aria-expanded="false" class="collapsed collapselink" 
        data-parent="#accordion" data-toggle="collapse" href="#collapseA4" role="button"><strong><font size="3px">&nabla;</font>&nbsp; &nbsp;Does enrolling in the ACP keep participants safe?</strong></a></h4>
        </div>

        <div aria-labelledby="headingA4" class="panel-collapse collapse" id="collapseA4" role="tabpanel">
          <div class="panel-body">
          <p>ACP participation does not guarantee safety. If used correctly, the ACP can be a valuable addition to an overall 
          safety plan. The ACP adds to, and does not substitute for, a comprehensive safety plan.</p></div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="panel panel-default">
        <div class="panel-heading" id="headingA5" role="tab">
        <h4 class="panel-title"><a aria-controls="collapseA5" aria-expanded="false" class="collapsed collapselink" 
        data-parent="#accordion" data-toggle="collapse" href="#collapseA5" role="button"><strong><font size="3px">&nabla;</font>&nbsp; &nbsp;Must government agencies accept the substitute address on an ACP Authorization Card?</strong></a></h4>
        </div>

        <div aria-labelledby="headingA5" class="panel-collapse collapse" id="collapseA5" role="tabpanel">
          <div class="panel-body">
          <p>Yes, with very few exceptions. State and local government agencies must accept the ACP substitute address when 
          presented a valid ACP authorization card. They cannot require a participant to disclose their actual address.</p></div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="panel panel-default">
        <div class="panel-heading" id="headingA6" role="tab">
        <h4 class="panel-title"><a aria-controls="collapseA6" aria-expanded="false" class="collapsed collapselink" 
        data-parent="#accordion" data-toggle="collapse" href="#collapseA6" role="button"><strong><font size="3px">&nabla;</font>&nbsp; &nbsp;Can ACP participants vote?</strong></a></h4>
        </div>

        <div aria-labelledby="headingA6" class="panel-collapse collapse" id="collapseA6" role="tabpanel">
          <div class="panel-body">
          <p>Yes. ACP participants receive ACP voter registration information and ACP specific voting forms when they enroll. 
          ACP participants should not register to vote online or at any state government office. 
          This includes:</p>
          <ul>
	    <li>Department of Licensing</li>
	    <li>Department of Social and Health Services</li>
	    <li>Health Benefits Exchange Office</li>
          </ul></div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="panel panel-default">
        <div class="panel-heading" id="headingA7" role="tab">
        <h4 class="panel-title"><a aria-controls="collapseA7" aria-expanded="false" class="collapsed collapselink" 
        data-parent="#accordion" data-toggle="collapse" href="#collapseA7" role="button"><strong><font size="3px">&nabla;</font>&nbsp; &nbsp;Where can I use the ACP substitute address?</strong></a></h4>
        </div>

        <div aria-labelledby="headingA7" class="panel-collapse collapse" id="collapseA7" role="tabpanel">
          <div class="panel-body">
            <p>All state or local agencies, and most private businesses accept the ACP substitute address.&nbsp;</p>
            <p>This includes:</p>
            <ul>
	      <li>Voting Registration</li>
	      <li>Department of Licensing</li>
	      <li>Marriage Records</li>
	      <li>Banks</li>
	      <li>Public Libraries</li>
	      <li>Washington Court System</li>
	      <li>Social Services</li>
	      <li>Police Departments</li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="panel panel-default">
        <div class="panel-heading" id="headingA8" role="tab">
        <h4 class="panel-title"><a aria-controls="collapseA8" aria-expanded="false" class="collapsed collapselink" 
        data-parent="#accordion" data-toggle="collapse" href="#collapseA8" role="tab"><strong><font size="3px">&nabla;</font>&nbsp; &nbsp; Confidential vs. Non-Confidential</strong></a></h4>
        </div>

        <div aria-labelledby="headingA8" class="panel-collapse collapse" id="collapseA8" role="tabpanel">
          <div class="panel-body">
            <h3><strong>Confidential Information </strong></h3>

            <ul>
              <li>Actual street address</li>
              <li>Mailing address, if different</li>
              <li>City of residence</li>
              <li>County of residence</li>
              <li>Voting precinct</li>
              <li>Unique identifying information related to a participant's home, work, or school address</li>
            </ul>

            <h3><strong>Non-Confidential Information</strong></h3>

            <ul>
              <li>ACP participation status</li>
              <li>A participant's substitute address</li>
              <li>State of residence</li>
              <li>Records created before ACP enrollment</li>
              <li>Information voluntarily provided by the participant to a state or local government agency</li>
            </ul>
            <p>&nbsp;</p>
          </div>
        </div>
