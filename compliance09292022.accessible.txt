  <header>
    <p><strong>The purpose of the ACP is to enable state and local agencies to respond 
    for requests for public records without disclosing the actual location of the program participant. </strong></p>
  </header>
  
  <main>

    <section id="State-and-local-government">
    <div aria-multiselectable="true" class="panel-group" id="accordionState-and-local-government" role="tablist">

      <div class="panel panel-default">
        <div class="panel-heading" id="headingState-and-local-government" role="tab">
        <h2 class="panel-title"><a aria-controls="collapseState-and-local-government" aria-expanded="true" data-parent="#accordionState-and-local-government" class="collapselink"
        data-toggle="collapse" href="#collapseState-and-local-government" role="button"><strong><font size="3px">&nabla;</font>&nbsp; &nbsp; State and Local Government</strong></a></h2>
        </div>

        <div aria-labelledby="headingState-and-local-government" class="panel-collapse collapse" id="collapseState-and-local-government" role="tabpanel">
          <div class="panel-body">
          <p><strong>&shy;</strong>Pursuant of 
          <a class="linkydink" href="https://app.leg.wa.gov/RCW/default.aspx?cite=40.24.050" target="_blank">RCW 40.24.050</a>, a program participant may request 
          Washington state and local agencies, law enforcement and the courts to use his or her designated Address Confidentiality 
          Program (ACP) address. When creating or modifying a new public record, state and local government agencies shall accept 
          the address designated by the secretary of state as a participant's home, work, or school address unless the 
          secretary has determined that:</p>

          <ul>
	    <li>The agency has a bona fide statutory or administrative requirement for the use of the address which would otherwise be confidential; and</li>
	    <li>The address will be used only for statutory administrative purposes</li>
          </ul>

          <p>Sample <a class="linkydink" href="/_assets/acp/new%20acp%20authorization%20card%20images.pdf" target="_blank">ACP Authorization Card</a></p>

    <section id="Schools">
    <div aria-multiselectable="true" class="panel-group" id="accordionSchools" role="tablist">

      <div class="panel panel-default">
        <div class="panel-heading" id="headingSchools" role="tab">
        <h2 class="panel-title"><a aria-controls="collapseSchools" aria-expanded="true" data-parent="#accordionSchools" class="collapselink"
        data-toggle="collapse" href="#collapseSchools" role="button"><strong><font size="3px">&nabla;</font>&nbsp; &nbsp; Schools</strong></a></h2>
        </div>

        <div aria-labelledby="headingSchools" class="panel-collapse collapse" id="collapseSchools" role="tabpanel">
          <div class="panel-body">
            <p>Public schools are required to accept and use the substitute address as the legal address of an Address 
            Confidentiality Program (ACP) family when presented with an ACP authorization card. All student records must 
            show only the ACP substitute address to protect students. The location of the actual residence is not permitted.</p>

            <p>Schools are allowed to make a copy of the card and are encouraged to do so to ensure student safety. Schools 
            are also encouraged to contact ACP to verify that the family is actively enrolled in the ACP program.</p>

            <p>The ACP participant is encouraged to provide the school with emergency contact information for the child or children. 
            If an ACP participant requests transportation, the parent or guardian must provide the drop-off and pick-up location.</p>

            <p>The school is authorized to use the ACP substitute address for regular First-Class correspondence and forwarding records.</p>

            <p><strong>School District Eligibility</strong></p>

            <p>Upon request from the family or the school, Address Confidentiality Program (ACP) staff may send a letter verifying the 
            residence is within district boundaries. Staff will mail the verification letter to the ACP participant and ask that the 
            participant redact their residential address from the letter. The letter can be shared with the district or school.</p>

            <p>Sample <a class="linkydink" href="/_assets/acp/new%20acp%20authorization%20card%20images.pdf" target="_blank">ACP Authorization Card</a></p>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
    </section>

          accept the substitute address.</p>

    <section id="FAQs">
    <div aria-multiselectable="true" class="panel-group" id="accordionFAQs" role="tablist">
      <div class="panel panel-default">
        <div class="panel-heading" id="headingFAQs" role="tab">
        <h2 class="panel-title"><a aria-controls="collapseFAQs" aria-expanded="true" data-parent="#accordionFAQs" class="collapselink"
        data-toggle="collapse" href="#collapseFAQs" role="button"><strong><font size="3px">&nabla;</font>&nbsp; &nbsp; FAQs</strong></a></h2>
        </div>

        <div aria-labelledby="headingFAQs" class="panel-collapse collapse" id="collapseFAQs" role="tabpanel">
          <div class="panel-body">
            
            <h3>Can agencies verify ACP participant status?</h3>
            <p>Yes.&nbsp; Agencies are encouraged to ask for, and make a copy of, a participant&rsquo;s ACP Authorization Card.&nbsp; 
            They can also call the ACP at (360) 753-2972 to confirm their status.&nbsp; <em>The ACP will not release any other information 
            about a participant other than their status in the program.</em></p>

            <h3>Can agencies make a copy of the ACP Authorization Card?</h3>
            <p>Yes.&nbsp; Agencies are encouraged to make a copy of a participant&rsquo;s ACP Authorization Card.</p>

            <h3>Must state and local government agencies accept the substitute address reflected on an ACP Authorization Card?</h3>
            <p>Yes, with very few exceptions. State, and local government agencies must accept the ACP substitute address when 
            presented a valid ACP authorization card. They cannot require a participant to disclose their actual address.</p>

            <h3>Can ACP participants receive registered mail or subpoenas?</h3>
            <p>Yes. The ACP accepts personal and legal mail. The ACP can receive such mail on behalf of a participant and forward it to them. &nbsp;This is treated as a participant receiving the mail.</p>
